Modelos e algoritmos inteligentes para apoio à tomada de decisão em Logística 4.0
Descrição: O presente projeto trata do desenvolvimento de algoritmos eficientes para resolver problemas de otimização de natureza logística. Muitos desses problemas são NP-Difíceis e, portanto, não podem ser resolvidos em tempo polinomial. Contudo, ainda há necessidade de resolvê-los, sendo duas as abordagens mais comumente utilizadas: (i) as heurísticas encontram soluções em tempos computacionais aceitáveis, mas sem garantir otimalidade; e (ii) os métodos exatos garantem a otimalidade ao custo computacional exponencial no pior caso. Apesar de suas limitações, estas últimas podem funcionar bem para problemas de pequeno e médio porte e também servem para avaliar abordagens heurísticas. O projeto visa desenvolver modelos e algoritmos que tragam avanços ao estado-da-arte na resolução de Problemas de Otimização Combinatória (POC) no contexto logístico que envolvem a tomada de decisões para melhor utilização de recursos, garantia de satisfação de clientes e aumento de competitividade. Especificamente, serão tratados problemas de logística externa, como Roteamento de Veículos, e de logística interna, como Dimensionamento de Lotes e Escalonamento de Tarefas. Assim sendo, pretende-se investigar como resolver eficientemente determinados POCs de natureza logística sob os pontos de vista de qualidade de solução e de tempo computacional. Apesar dos recentes avanços científicos na resolução de tais problemas serem expressivos, ainda há lacunas metodológicas a serem exploradas que podem contribuir para o êxito das abordagens de otimização a serem propostas durante a execução do projeto. As técnicas exatas que serão investigadas incluem principalmente algoritmos baseados em Programação Inteira. Já as abordagens baseadas em (meta-)heurísticas também serão desenvolvidas de modo a produzir soluções de qualidade em tempo computacional reduzido (EDITAL N 19/2022 PROGRAMA DE APOIO A NÚCLEOS EM CONSOLIDAÇÃO DO ESTADO DA PARAÍBA)..

Rich vehicle routing problems: models and algorithms for deterministic and stochastic variants
Descrição: In the past few years, the development of computer technology together with methodological advances in Operations Research has motivated the investigation of more complex variants of the Vehicle Routing Problem. These variants are often inspired by real-world applications and they often include challenging objective functions and constraints. In the literature, such problems are referred to as Rich Vehicle Routing Problems (RVRPs). The interest on RVRPs has recently increased and especially focused on deterministic variants, although most realistic applications are typically characterized by a high level of uncertainty. Indeed, logistics managers must be prepared to make complex decisions without complete information. On the other hand, taking uncertainties into account adds more challenges to modeling and solving the involved problems. This research project aims at studying RVRPs that arise on commercial and humanitarian logistics, under deterministic and stochastic perspectives. We intend to develop new mathematical formulations and exact, heuristic and hybrid methods for RVRPs that capture real-world features and are computationally efficient to aid decision making in practice. This project has the potential of establishing and consolidating a collaboration between research groups from the states of Para#305;#769;ba and São Paulo, in an effort to effectively address challenging practical problems, with scientific and technological outcomes for both states..

Optimizing Freight Transportation: Models and Algorithms
Descrição: In production industry, the transportation of the freight easily amounts to 10% of the total product cost, as documented, e.g., by the European Commission. Moreover, the largest amount of products are nowadays transported by road, and this increases not only costs, but also pollution, traffic jams, and accidents, considerably worsening the quality of life of the areas involved by the transportation process. Two main options are available to increase the efficiency of the transportation process. The first one is to build new infrastructures, such as highways, that speed up the connections between cities. Brazil is already investing much in this process, but this comes at a high cost. The second option is to optimize the transportation of freight, by using tools that allow companies and local authorities to perform their distribution process in the best possible way. This involves producing mathematical models that can replicate the behavior of the distribution systems, and developing algorithms that can solve the models and find the best way to perform the distribution. Clearly the optimization of freight transportation combines well with the process of building new highways, but has the advantage of requiring much cheaper costs. Moreover, the gains in efficiency that can be obtained are so important that the optimization of freight transportation is considered one of the key points for a sustainable logistics, as discussed by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development. The activity we propose in this three-year project has a twofold objective. The first one is that of producing new mathematical models and efficient algorithms for the solution of some important optimization problems arising in the field of freight transportation. In particular, we intend to develop solution tools that can be used by companies and local authorities to perform their distributions in the best possible ways, so reducing product cost, pollution, traffic jams, and accidents. More in details, we expect to produce solution tools that output precise distribution plans, that include the detailed scheduling of the activities (where and when products have to be loaded on a commercial vehicle/truck, and where and when they have to be unloaded) and the routing of the vehicles (which path should be used by each vehicle to perform its list of scheduled activities). The second objective is to transfer technology and scientific knowledge to the Operations Research group of the Universidade Federal da Paraíba. This group is young, well-motivated, and is growing fast, especially in the field of heuristic algorithms. These algorithms are usually very effective in the solution of large problems, but sometimes lack in accuracy and scientific rigor. The combination of heuristic with exact algorithms and mathematical models may lead to much better results, allowing to obtain at the same time solutions that are more accurate and more robust. The collaboration with a talented team of researchers from abroad, specialized in exact algorithms and mathematical models, would allow the Brazilian group to enrich their capabilities. It would fasten up their development and improve their scientific independence, increasing their potential to publish articles in international journals and to solve real-world problems, thus contributing to the wealth of the Brazilian north-east area. Algoritmos eficientes para resolução de problemas de roteamento de veículos
Descrição: O Problema de Roteamento de Veículos (PRV) é um problema clássico de otimização combinatória proposto no final da década de 1950, sendo ainda um dos mais estudados na área de Pesquisa Operacional. O elevado interesse no PRV é devido a sua importância prática, bem como a dificuldade em resolvê-lo. Este projeto trata de algoritmos eficientes para PRVs com ênfase em variantes propostas recentemente na literatura, a saber: PRV que leva em conta emissões de poluição (PRV-Pol), PRV Clusterizado (PRV-Clu), Problema do Caixeiro Viajante com Limites de Carga (PCVLC) e Problema de Roteamento e Scheduling de Advogados. Apesar do escopo principal do projeto estar voltado para variantes recentes do PRV, há também intenção de estudar as variantes consideradas clássicas como o PRV com Frota Heterogêna (PRFVH) e extensões, PRV com Entregas Fracionárias (PRVEF), além de aplicações reais do PRV na indústria. Os metódos de resolução a serem utilizados são baseados em abordagens heurísticas, exatas e híbridas. A equipe deste projeto é composta por pesquisadores de diversas instituições federais do Brasil, tais como UFF, UFV e UFOP, além de contar com pesquisadores de instituições internacionais especialistas em roteamento de veículos.
